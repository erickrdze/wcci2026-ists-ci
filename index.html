Motivation & Scope

The accelerating transformation of transport systems is creating unprecedented opportunities for innovation at the intersection of artificial intelligence, optimization, and mobility. Advances in sensing, communication, and automation are generating vast amounts of data and increasingly complex decision problems — from urban traffic control and multimodal coordination to autonomous driving and last-mile logistics.

In this context, Computational Intelligence (CI) provides a powerful set of tools to model, learn, and optimize within dynamic and uncertain environments. The session explores how CI can enable intelligent, efficient, and sustainable transport, integrating data-driven learning, predictive control, and adaptive optimization to support real-time decision-making.

We welcome both methodological and applied contributions that bridge theory and implementation, and encourage collaboration across AI, operations research, transport engineering, and urban planning.

Topics of Interest

  • Traffic management, control, and roadworks planning
  • Vehicle routing, fleet optimization, and last-mile logistics
  • Demand prediction, mobility patterns, and dynamic pricing
  • Public transport synchronization and DRT integration
  • Digital twins, simulation frameworks, and multi-agent systems
  • AI for autonomous, connected, and shared vehicles
  • Multi-objective and real-time optimization for sustainable mobility
  • Decision-support systems for multimodal and urban logistics planning
