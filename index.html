Abstract

The transport and mobility sector is changing faster than ever. Digitalization, automation, and the increasing demand for sustainability are transforming how people and goods move. These shifts are reshaping both urban and interurban transport, with data-driven and connected infrastructures becoming the backbone of modern mobility. However, this evolution also brings new challenges — congestion, safety, energy efficiency, accessibility, and equity — that demand smarter and more adaptive systems. Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Computational Intelligence (CI) play a crucial role in addressing these issues by providing tools for learning, optimization, and decision-making in complex and dynamic environments.

This Special Session aims to bring together researchers and practitioners working on AI and optimization methods for transport systems and logistics. The goal is to create a multidisciplinary space where diverse perspectives — from algorithmic design and simulation to deployment and policy — can converge and exchange ideas. We particularly welcome contributions that combine learning and optimization to improve the design, control, and management of transport networks. Overall, the session underscores the growing importance of computational intelligence in shaping intelligent, efficient, and sustainable mobility ecosystems, aligning naturally with the themes of IEEE CEC, FUZZ-IEEE, and IJCNN.

Motivation & Scope

The increasing digitalization of transport, the push for sustainability, and the emergence of connected and automated mobility are redefining how people and goods move. Computational Intelligence provides a powerful toolkit — learning, optimization, and simulation — to design, manage, and operate modern transport systems in dynamic and uncertain contexts.

The session focuses on AI- and optimization-based approaches that bridge theory and implementation. We welcome methodological advances, comparative studies, simulation-based frameworks, and real-world applications that demonstrate impact on decision-making and operations.

Topics of Interest

  • Traffic management, control, and roadworks planning
  • Vehicle routing, fleet optimization, and last-mile logistics
  • Demand prediction, mobility patterns, and dynamic pricing
  • Public transport synchronization and DRT integration
  • Digital twins and multi-agent simulation of transport systems
  • AI for autonomous, connected, and shared vehicles
  • Multi-objective and real-time optimization for sustainable mobility
  • Decision-support systems for multimodal and urban logistics planning
